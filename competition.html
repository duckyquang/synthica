Synthica Competition

Showcase your research on a global stage and compete with the best high school researchers worldwide.

Timeline

Competition Timeline

The Synthica Competition consists of two main rounds: Regional and Global. Teams from each school (maximum 3 teams per school) progress through multiple stages of evaluation and mentorship.

Regional Round

Research Brief Submission

Teams (maximum 3 per school) submit briefs of their research ideas. Each team outlines their research question, methodology, and expected outcomes.

Top 50 Selection

The top 50 teams are selected and invited to the next phase. These teams receive mentorship assignment and begin working with their assigned mentors (university students, PhD students, etc.).

Mentorship & First Draft

Teams work closely with their assigned mentors to develop their research. They must submit their first draft by the specified deadline.

Top 25 Selection

From the Top 50, the top 25 teams are selected and invited to the Synthica Continental Science Fair. These teams prepare for in-person presentations and judging.

Continental Science Fair

The Top 25 teams present their research at the Continental Science Fair. Judges visit teams, evaluate presentations, and select the top performers. Note: All Top 50 teams can still complete their research and get published in the Synthica Journal.

Global Round

Top 3 from Each Region

The top 3 winning teams from each region advance to the global stage, representing the best research from their continent.

Synthica Grand Final Thesis Defense

International finalists participate in the Grand Final Thesis Defense round, presenting and defending their research before a distinguished panel of expert judges from top universities worldwide.

Winners Announced

Winners are announced and celebrated. Top researchers receive prizes, mentorship opportunities, publication recognition, and other prestigious awards.
