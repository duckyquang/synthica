Synthica Competition

Showcase your research on a global stage and compete with the best high school researchers worldwide.

Timeline

Competition Timeline

The Synthica Competition consists of two main rounds: Regional and Global. Teams from each school (maximum 3 teams per school) progress through multiple stages of evaluation and mentorship.

Regional Round

Research Brief Submission

Teams (maximum 3 per school) submit briefs of their research ideas. Each team outlines their research question, methodology, and expected outcomes.

Top 50 Selection

The top 50 teams are selected and invited to the next phase. These teams receive mentorship assignment and begin working with their assigned mentors (university students, PhD students, etc.).

Mentorship & First Draft

Teams work closely with their assigned mentors to develop their research. They must submit their first draft by the specified deadline.

Top 25 Selection

From the Top 50, the top 25 teams are selected and invited to the Synthica Continental Science Fair. These teams prepare for in-person presentations and judging.

Continental Science Fair

The Top 25 teams present their research at the Continental Science Fair. Judges visit teams, evaluate presentations, and select the top performers. Note: All Top 50 teams can still complete their research and get published in the Synthica Journal.

Global Round

Top 3 from Each Region

The top 3 winning teams from each region advance to the global stage, representing the best research from their continent.

Synthica Grand Final Thesis Defense

International finalists participate in the Grand Final Thesis Defense round, presenting and defending their research before a distinguished panel of expert judges from top universities worldwide.

Winners Announced

Winners are announced and celebrated. Top researchers receive prizes, mentorship opportunities, publication recognition, and other prestigious awards.

Synthica Research Competition - International High School Research Competition

The Synthica Research Competition is an international research competition designed for high school students to showcase their research projects, compete with peers worldwide, and win research awards, research scholarships, and research grants. This free research competition provides students with opportunities to present research papers, demonstrate research skills, receive research feedback, and gain research recognition. The research competition includes regional research competition rounds and an international research competition round, with comprehensive research competition timeline, research competition rubric, and research competition prizes.

Research Competition Structure

The research competition follows a structured format with regional research competition rounds leading to the international research competition round. Students participate in research competition stages including research project submission, research presentation, research evaluation, and research competition judging. The research competition rubric evaluates research methodology, research quality, research innovation, research presentation, and research impact. Research competition winners receive research awards, research scholarships, research grants, and research recognition.

Research Competition Benefits

Participating in the Synthica Research Competition offers numerous benefits including research project showcase opportunities, research skills demonstration, research feedback from judges, research recognition and awards, research scholarship opportunities, research grant possibilities, research networking with peers, research collaboration connections, research career development, research portfolio building, research presentation experience, research paper evaluation, research methodology feedback, and research competition participation credentials.

Research Competition Timeline and Process

The research competition follows a detailed timeline with specific research competition deadlines, research competition dates, and research competition schedule. Students must complete research competition registration, research competition application, research project submission, research presentation preparation, and research competition participation according to the research competition timeline. The research competition process includes research competition evaluation, research competition judging, research competition feedback, research competition results announcement, and research competition awards ceremony.

Research Competition Eligibility and Requirements

High school students worldwide are eligible to participate in the research competition. Research competition requirements include research project completion, research paper submission, research presentation preparation, research competition application, and research competition registration. The research competition is open to all research disciplines, research fields, and research topics. Research competition rules, research competition guidelines, and research competition criteria ensure fair research competition evaluation and research competition judging.

Research Competition Prizes and Awards

The research competition offers valuable research prizes including research awards, research scholarships, research grants, research recognition, and research competition certificates. Research competition winners, research competition finalists, and research competition participants receive various research competition benefits. Research competition prizes recognize research excellence, research innovation, research quality, research presentation skills, and research impact. Research competition awards are presented at the research competition awards ceremony following research competition judging and research competition evaluation.
